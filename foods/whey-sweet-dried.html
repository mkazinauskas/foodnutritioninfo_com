Completeness score of Whey, sweet, dried is 44.

A Completeness Score between 0 and 100 is a relative indication of how complete the food is with respect to these nutrients. Protein quality for Whey, sweet, dried
100 grams

Protein quality score of Whey, sweet, dried is 102.

An Amino Acid Score of 100 or higher indicates a complete or high-quality protein. If the Amino Acid Score is less than 100, a link is provided to complementary sources of protein. By combining complementary proteins, you may be able to increase the overall quality of the protein you consume.

Pros and Cons of Whey, sweet, dried
100 grams

The good: This food is low in Saturated Fat, and very low in Cholesterol. It is also a good source of Vitamin B12, Pantothenic Acid, Magnesium, Potassium and Selenium, and a very good source of Riboflavin, Calcium and Phosphorus.

The bad: This food is high in Sodium, and a large portion of the calories in this food come from sugars.

Glycemic load of Whey, sweet, dried is: 49

Glycemic load is a way of expressing a food or meal's effect on blood-sugar levels. A typical target for total Estimated Glycemic Load is 100 or less per day.
