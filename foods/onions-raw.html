Nutrient balance indicator for Onions, raw

Completeness score of Onions, raw is 53.

A Completeness Score between 0 and 100 is a relative indication of how complete the food is with respect to these nutrients. Protein quality for Onions, raw
100 grams

Protein quality score of Onions, raw is 22.

An Amino Acid Score of 100 or higher indicates a complete or high-quality protein. If the Amino Acid Score is less than 100, a link is provided to complementary sources of protein. By combining complementary proteins, you may be able to increase the overall quality of the protein you consume.

Pros and Cons of Onions, raw
100 grams

The good: This food is very low in Saturated Fat, Cholesterol and Sodium. It is also a good source of Dietary Fiber, Vitamin B6, Folate, Potassium and Manganese, and a very good source of Vitamin C.

The bad: A large portion of the calories in this food come from sugars.

Glycemic load of Onions, raw is: 3

Glycemic load is a way of expressing a food or meal's effect on blood-sugar levels. A typical target for total Estimated Glycemic Load is 100 or less per day.
