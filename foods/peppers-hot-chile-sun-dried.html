Pros and Cons of Peppers, hot chile, sun-dried
100 grams

The good: This food is low in Saturated Fat and Sodium, and very low in Cholesterol. It is also a good source of Vitamin C, Niacin, Vitamin B6, Iron, Potassium and Manganese, and a very good source of Dietary Fiber, Vitamin A, Vitamin K and Riboflavin.

The bad: A large portion of the calories in this food come from sugars.

Glycemic load of Peppers, hot chile, sun-dried is: 21

Glycemic load is a way of expressing a food or meal's effect on blood-sugar levels. A typical target for total Estimated Glycemic Load is 100 or less per day.
